The Nairobi-Paris route (≈6,400 km) is a major Africa-Europe corridor, served by Air France direct, KLM (via Amsterdam), Lufthansa (via Frankfurt), and Kenya Airways (with codeshare partners). EC 261 applies for EU-airline-operated flights.

Compensation amount

  • Distance Nairobi-Paris: ≈6,400 km direct
  • EC 261 bracket: >3,500 km
  • Compensation: €600 per passenger
  • Conditions: delay 3h+ at Paris CDG (final destination)

Kenya Airways codeshare nuance

If your ticket is sold as Kenya Airways but operated by an EU partner (KLM, Air France), EC 261 applies. Check the boarding pass: the "operated by" mention indicates the operating carrier responsible for compensation.

Common delay causes on Nairobi-Paris

  • Nairobi (JKIA) congestion: routine operational issue, not extraordinary.
  • Crew rotation: internal management, not extraordinary.
  • Technical issues: per Wallentin-Hermann, not extraordinary.
  • Weather at intermediate hub: only valid if NOTAM-confirmed.
  • Slot delays at Paris CDG: may be extraordinary if true ATC event.

How to claim

  1. Gather documents: PNR, boarding pass, photo of arrival time at Paris CDG.
  2. Submit online claim on the operating airline's website (Air France, KLM, Lufthansa).
  3. Provide IBAN: EU account is fastest, Kenyan account also accepted.
  4. Wait 30-60 days.
  5. Escalate to DGAC France or Dutch ACM if refused.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Nairobi-Paris route covered by EC 261?
Yes if operated by an EU airline: Air France direct, KLM via Amsterdam, Lufthansa via Frankfurt, Brussels Airlines via Brussels. Kenya Airways (non-EU): EC 261 applies only on EU-departing flights, not Nairobi-Paris.
Distance and compensation Nairobi-Paris?
Nairobi-Paris direct ≈6,400 km. EC 261 bracket >3,500 km. Compensation: €600 per passenger if delayed 3h+ at Paris. Family of 4 = €2,400 total.
Kenya Airways Nairobi-Paris — am I entitled?
Not directly under EC 261 (Kenya Airways is a non-EU carrier, Nairobi is non-EU departure). For Paris-Nairobi return, EC 261 does apply. The Montreal Convention covers baggage. Kenyan law and Kenya Airways' own terms apply for delays — less protective than EC 261.
KLM Nairobi-Amsterdam-Paris delayed — what compensation?
€600 on a single ticket. KLM is an EU airline so EC 261 applies regardless of departure point on EU-arrival flights. Total Nairobi-Paris distance counts (~6,400 km), delay measured at Paris.
How long does it take to recover compensation on this route?
Typically 60-150 days. Air France and KLM are faster (60-90 days), Lufthansa slower (90-120). With escalation to DGAC, expect 4-6 months. Worth the wait — €600 per passenger on a popular route.
