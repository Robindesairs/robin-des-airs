Ethiopian Airlines delayed flight: your EC 261 rights

By the Robin des Airs team · Published on January 12, 2026 · Updated on January 28, 2026

Key takeaways

  • Is Ethiopian Airlines covered by EC 261? — Partially. Ethiopia is NOT in the EU. EC 261 applies if the flight departs from an EU airport (e.g. Paris-Addis Ababa). It does NOT apply for flights from Addis Ababa to the EU on Ethiopian Airlines…
  • Paris-Addis Ababa on Ethiopian Airlines delayed — what compensation? — €600 per passenger if delayed 3h+ at Addis Ababa (distance ≈5,600 km, >3,500 km). Plus right to care during the wait (meals, hotel, transport).
  • Ethiopian Airlines is a Star Alliance partner. Does that change anything? — No, for EC 261. Star Alliance partnerships affect frequent flyer miles and lounge access, not EC 261 rights. The applicability depends solely on departure airport and operating carrier nationality.

Ethiopian Airlines is Africa's largest carrier and a Star Alliance member. It operates a major hub at Addis Ababa Bole, connecting Europe to Africa, Asia and the Americas. EC 261 applies for EU-departing flights, regardless of the airline being non-EU.

Compensation amounts

For Ethiopian Airlines flights covered by EC 261:

  • Paris-Addis Ababa (≈5,600 km): €600 if delayed 3h+
  • Brussels-Addis Ababa (≈5,800 km): €600
  • Frankfurt-Addis Ababa (≈5,400 km): €600
  • Single ticket via Addis to onward African destination: based on total distance — typically €600

The "weather at Addis" defense

Ethiopian Airlines frequently cites "weather at Addis Ababa" to refuse compensation. Carefully verify:

  • Were the conditions truly extraordinary? Highland weather in Ethiopia is generally stable.
  • Were other flights operating normally that day? If yes, the "weather" defense is weak.
  • Can they produce a NOTAM showing airport closure or significant weather event?

Without documentary proof, the refusal is contestable.

Claim process against Ethiopian Airlines

  1. Online form: ethiopianairlines.com → Customer Relations
  2. Documents: PNR, boarding pass, IBAN, passenger ID
  3. Wait 60-90 days: Ethiopian Airlines is slower than European carriers
  4. If refused: escalate to the NEB of the EU departure country (DGAC France, LBA Germany, etc.). Ethiopian Airlines complies with EU enforcement to keep its routes.
  5. Last resort: small claims court in EU jurisdiction

Star Alliance partnership: minor impact

Ethiopian is part of Star Alliance with Lufthansa, SWISS, Brussels Airlines, etc. If your ticket was booked with a Star Alliance partner (e.g. Lufthansa) but operated by Ethiopian, the claim is against Ethiopian (the operating carrier). The ticket-issuing airline may help facilitate the claim.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Ethiopian Airlines covered by EC 261?
Partially. Ethiopia is NOT in the EU. EC 261 applies if the flight departs from an EU airport (e.g. Paris-Addis Ababa). It does NOT apply for flights from Addis Ababa to the EU on Ethiopian Airlines (non-EU departure + non-EU operator).
Paris-Addis Ababa on Ethiopian Airlines delayed — what compensation?
€600 per passenger if delayed 3h+ at Addis Ababa (distance ≈5,600 km, >3,500 km). Plus right to care during the wait (meals, hotel, transport).
Ethiopian Airlines is a Star Alliance partner. Does that change anything?
No, for EC 261. Star Alliance partnerships affect frequent flyer miles and lounge access, not EC 261 rights. The applicability depends solely on departure airport and operating carrier nationality.
Multi-leg Brussels-Addis-Lagos on Ethiopian: who is responsible if delayed?
On a single ticket, EC 261 covers the journey. Brussels-Addis is EU-departing, so the leg is covered. If you arrive at Lagos 3h+ late, compensation is calculated on the total distance (~7,500 km = €600). Ethiopian Airlines is the operating carrier and responsible.
How to claim against Ethiopian Airlines?
Online via ethiopianairlines.com customer relations or email. They are slow (60-90 days response). If refused, escalate to the National Enforcement Body of the EU departure country. Ethiopian Airlines complies with EU enforcement orders to maintain EU operations.
