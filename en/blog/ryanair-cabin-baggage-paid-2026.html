In 2026, the bill is clear: Ryanair, Wizz Air, Transavia and Vueling have made cabin baggage paid beyond a small backpack. At the boarding gate, a bag not paid online costs up to €60. It's legal, contractual — and it has become one of the main ancillary revenue streams for low-cost carriers.

Good news: with the right reflexes, you avoid this extra cost. And if the airline goes beyond its terms (flight delayed, cancelled, or compliant baggage refused), you have concrete remedies.

What changed in 2025-2026

Until 2018, Ryanair allowed two free cabin bags (large + small). In 2024, it dropped to one standard cabin bag. Since 2025, the rule has tightened:

The 3 cabin baggage categories in 2026

1. The small underseat backpack — free

This is your absolute right. An urban backpack (school bag, compact gym bag, laptop sleeve) fits the 40 × 20 × 25 cm allowance on Ryanair. No charge is possible as long as you stay within these dimensions.

Practical tip: compressible hiking backpacks (Osprey Daylite, Decathlon Forclaz 30L) easily fit the sizer once well packed. A rigid backpack 2 cm too big will be refused.

2. The standard cabin bag — paid (€12-35)

Wheeled cabin case 55 × 40 × 20 cm, up to 10 kg. This is the middle option. Price varies with route and timing: the later you buy, the more expensive. In 2026, expect €12-15 for an intra-European flight booked early, €30-35 for a peak-time flight booked the day before.

3. Hold baggage — always paid

10 kg or 20 kg hold bag. Essential for long trips or diaspora travellers bringing back products. Always book online: paying at the counter is 50-80% more expensive.

5 reflexes to avoid the €60 surcharge

  1. Check your bag dimensions BEFORE leaving for the airport — with a tape measure, at home. Not at the gate.
  2. Add the standard cabin bag to your booking at purchase — it's 3 to 5 times cheaper than at the gate.
  3. Travelling as a couple on Ryanair: only one of you pays for the standard cabin bag; you share. Saves €12-35.
  4. Hybrid bag: a large soft backpack, compressed, fits the underseat allowance once packed tight. Test it at home first.
  5. Online check-in is mandatory: forgetting online check-in = €55 extra on Ryanair, on top of the baggage. Do it as soon as it opens (24-48h before flight).

What if you are charged €60 even though your bag is compliant?

It happens — especially at the end of the boarding queue, when the agent tries to empty saturated overhead bins. If you are certain your bag fits the allowance:

  1. Demand a public measurement against the airline's official sizer (present at counter and gate).
  2. Film or photograph the bag inside the sizer. This is your main evidence.
  3. Keep all tickets: boarding pass, €60 receipt, gate ticket.
  4. Within 7 days of the flight, write to the airline to claim a refund (registered letter + customer service email).
  5. If refused: free MTV mediation (Travel and Tourism Mediation) in France, or DGAC for France-departing flights, or the equivalent National Enforcement Body (NEB) in your EU country.
Common case: Ryanair charges baggage at the gate even though the bag is compliant, because the agent decides to optimise loading. Photo inside the sizer = you win. Without a photo, it's your word against theirs.

And if the flight is delayed or cancelled: €250-600 compensation

Regulation (EC) 261/2004 applies to all airlines operating to or from the EU, including Ryanair, Wizz, Transavia, Vueling. Low-cost doesn't mean low-rights.

  • Arrival delay 3h+ on intra-EU flight: €250 (≤1500 km) or €400 (>1500 km) per passenger
  • Cancellation less than 14 days before flight without extraordinary circumstances: same scale
  • Overbooking / denied boarding: €250 to €600 + full ticket refund

For a Paris-Marrakech flight delayed by 4 hours on Ryanair, that's €400 per passenger. A family of 4 = €1,600. Compare that with the €60 they tried to charge you for the baggage.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the maximum free cabin baggage size on Ryanair in 2026?
40 × 20 × 25 cm — the size of a small backpack. Any larger bag must be added to your booking for €12-35 in advance, otherwise it will be charged €60 at the boarding gate.
Do Wizz Air and Transavia apply the same rules?
Yes, in spirit. Wizz Air allows 40 × 30 × 20 cm free; Transavia 40 × 30 × 20 cm too. Vueling 40 × 20 × 30 cm. Sizes differ by a few centimetres, but the principle is identical: one small underseat bag free, the rest paid.
If I am charged €60 at the gate while my bag is compliant, can I contest it?
Yes. Ask the gate agent to publicly measure your bag against the airline's official sizer (present at gate). Photograph the result. If the charge is unjustified, file a written claim with the airline within 7 days, attaching photos, receipt and boarding pass. If refused: MTV mediation (Travel Mediation) or DGAC for France-departing flights.
Can my cabin bag be placed in the hold for free if the cabin is full?
Yes — and it is free. If you board late and the overhead bins are full, the airline will place your compliant cabin bag in the hold at no charge. This is different from oversized bags forced into the hold (which remain paid).
If my flight is delayed or cancelled, am I entitled to compensation even with a low-cost ticket?
Yes. Regulation (EC) 261/2004 applies to all airlines operating to or from the EU, low-cost included. Ryanair, Wizz, Transavia: a 3h+ delay or cancellation under 14 days = €250 to €600 per passenger depending on distance.
