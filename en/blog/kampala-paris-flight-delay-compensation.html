The Entebbe (EBB) — Paris (CDG/ORY) route is one of the longest indirect connections from the Great Lakes region to Western Europe. With around 6,400 km of great-circle distance and almost always a connecting flight in Brussels, Amsterdam, Istanbul, Addis Ababa or Nairobi, the risk of a 3-hour+ delay or missed connection is real. The good news: under EU passenger rights law, you are very often entitled to €600 compensation per passenger.

Which Kampala-Paris flights are covered by EC 261/2004?

Regulation (EC) No 261/2004 applies in two cases:

  1. The flight departs from an EU airport (e.g. Brussels-Entebbe return), OR
  2. The operating airline is EU-licensed (Brussels Airlines, KLM, Air France, Lufthansa, etc.) regardless of the departure point.

For a Kampala→Paris journey, you are covered if any one of these conditions is met. In practice:

How much compensation for a Kampala-Paris delay?

The compensation amount under Article 7 of EC 261/2004 depends on the great-circle distance between the initial departure and the final destination:

  • 3,500 km or more: €600 per passenger
  • 1,500 to 3,500 km: €400 per passenger
  • Under 1,500 km: €250 per passenger

Kampala (Entebbe) to Paris CDG = ~6,400 km. You are in the €600 bracket, period.

Per passenger, including children

Compensation applies to every passenger with a paid confirmed ticket, including children (infants under 2 without their own seat are excluded).

Example: family of 4 (2 adults + 2 children with seats) on a 5-hour delayed EBB-CDG via BRU = 4 × €600 = €2,400.

When are you entitled?

Delayed flight (Sturgeon ruling)

Since the Sturgeon v Condor ruling (CJEU, 19 November 2009, C-402/07), a delay of 3 hours or more at the final destination triggers the same compensation as a cancellation. You don't need the flight to be cancelled.

Cancelled flight

If your flight is cancelled less than 14 days before departure, compensation is due — unless the airline offers a comparable rerouting (arrival within ±2-4h of the original) or proves extraordinary circumstances.

Missed connection (Folkerts ruling)

If your ticket is single (one booking) and you missed your Brussels/Amsterdam connection due to the EBB leg being late, the delay is measured at your final destination (Paris), not at the connection. Folkerts v Air France (CJEU, C-11/11) is your friend here.

Denied boarding (overbooking)

If you have a confirmed reservation and are denied boarding (overbooking, downgrade…), compensation is due regardless of the airline's operational reason. Reference: Rodríguez Cachafeiro (CJEU, C-321/11).

When the airline can refuse: extraordinary circumstances

The only valid reason to refuse compensation is "extraordinary circumstances" — but case law has dramatically narrowed this exception:

Reason invoked by airlineExtraordinary?

In our experience, around 80% of refusal grounds invoked by airlines are contestable using CJEU case law.

What to do if your Kampala-Paris flight was delayed

  1. Keep your boarding passes and booking confirmation (PDF, email, screenshots).
  2. Note the actual arrival time at CDG/ORY (door opening at the gate, not landing time).
  3. Photograph the airport screens showing the delay or cancellation.
  4. Keep receipts for any meals, taxis or hotels you paid for during the wait.
  5. Don't accept vouchers or "compensation in miles" without checking — cash compensation under EC 261 cannot be replaced by vouchers without your explicit consent.
  6. Submit your claim within 5 years in France (depending on jurisdiction). Use our simulator or contact us via WhatsApp.

Practical tips for the Uganda-France diaspora

  • Take pictures of your boarding pass at Entebbe airport immediately — they fade quickly and stamps may be hard to read later.
  • Ask for a written statement at the customer service desk documenting the reason given for the delay/cancellation. This is gold for your claim.
  • If you're rerouted via a non-EU stop (Cairo, Addis, Nairobi…), keep all boarding passes — Folkerts + Wegener case law lets you claim for the entire single-ticket journey.
  • Check the operating carrier, not the marketing carrier. A Kenya Airways-marketed flight may be operated by KLM (codeshare) and vice versa. The operating airline is the one liable.
