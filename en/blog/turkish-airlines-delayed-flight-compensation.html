Turkish Airlines is a major non-EU carrier connecting Europe to Asia and Africa via Istanbul. The applicability of EC 261 depends on the direction of your flight — a critical detail most passengers ignore.

The EC 261 rule for non-EU carriers

Regulation (EC) 261/2004 applies to:

  1. Flights departing FROM an EU airport, regardless of the airline's nationality
  2. Flights arriving in the EU operated by an EU-licensed airline

Turkey is NOT in the EU. So:

  • Paris-Istanbul on Turkish Airlines → EC 261 applies (EU departure)
  • Istanbul-Paris on Turkish Airlines → EC 261 does NOT apply (non-EU departure + non-EU airline)

This is the most misunderstood rule. Many passengers wrongly assume that Istanbul-Paris is covered.

Multi-leg trips via Istanbul: how it works

If you bought a single ticket Paris → Istanbul → Bangkok on Turkish Airlines and arrive at Bangkok 4h late because the Paris-Istanbul leg was delayed:

  • Total distance Paris-Bangkok: ≈9,500 km
  • Compensation: €600 per passenger
  • The delay at the final destination counts, not the intermediate connection

If you bought TWO separate tickets (Paris-Istanbul, then Istanbul-Bangkok), only the EU-departing segment is covered (Paris-Istanbul = €400 if delayed 3h+ at Istanbul).

Turkish Airlines' classic strategies

  • First-time refusal: they often refuse the first claim, citing "operational reasons" or "exceptional circumstances" without proof.
  • Voucher offer: they push vouchers for future Turkish Airlines flights — refuse, demand cash.
  • Currency confusion: they sometimes offer in Turkish lira (TRY) at a poor exchange rate. Demand euros.
  • Slow processing: typical response time 2-4 months. Be patient and persistent.

Claim process

  1. Submit online claim via turkishairlines.com customer service or the mobile app.
  2. Required documents: PNR, boarding pass, ID, IBAN (European bank account).
  3. Wait 30-60 days: Turkish Airlines is slow but responsive.
  4. If refused: escalate to the DGAC (France), LBA (Germany), or NEB of the EU departure country. Turkish Airlines is regularly forced to pay by EU regulators.
  5. Last resort: court action in EU jurisdiction (Brussels jurisdiction is favourable as Turkish Airlines has assets in EU).

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Turkish Airlines covered by EC 261?
Partially. Turkey is NOT in the EU. EC 261 applies if the flight departs FROM an EU airport (e.g. Paris-Istanbul). It does NOT apply for flights from Istanbul to EU (e.g. Istanbul-Paris) when operated by Turkish Airlines, because it's a non-EU airline departing from outside the EU.
Istanbul-Paris on Turkish Airlines delayed — am I entitled to EC 261?
No, unfortunately. EC 261 protects EU-departing flights or EU-arriving flights operated by EU carriers. Istanbul-Paris on Turkish Airlines (non-EU departure + non-EU operator) is outside EC 261 scope. Turkish national law may apply, but is much less protective.
Paris-Istanbul on Turkish Airlines delayed — yes EC 261?
Yes. The flight departs from an EU airport (Paris-CDG). EC 261 applies regardless of the operating airline's nationality. You can claim €400 (1,500-3,500 km) if delayed 3h+ at Istanbul.
Paris-Istanbul-Bangkok with delay at Istanbul connection — how is compensation calculated?
If purchased as ONE ticket from Paris to Bangkok via Istanbul, compensation is based on the total Paris-Bangkok distance (~9,500 km = €600). If delayed at the final destination by 3h+. Two separate tickets = each segment treated independently.
How to claim against Turkish Airlines?
Online form on turkishairlines.com or via your booking. They often refuse first — escalate to the National Enforcement Body of the EU departure country (DGAC in France, LBA in Germany, etc.). Turkish Airlines is regularly sued in EU jurisdictions and forced to pay.
