Ryanair is a European airline (Irish-licensed): Regulation (EC) 261/2004 applies to all flights departing from an EU airport or operated by Ryanair into the EU. Same rules as Air France, KLM, Lufthansa — low-cost does not mean low-rights.

Conditions to claim against Ryanair

You are entitled to compensation if:

  1. Your arrival was delayed by 3 hours or more compared to the original schedule (CJEU Sturgeon ruling, 2009).
  2. The delay was not caused by extraordinary circumstances (weather, ATC strike, security).
  3. Your flight departed from an EU airport OR was operated by Ryanair into the EU.

Per passenger — applies to every paying passenger, including children. A family of 4 on a Dublin-Faro flight delayed 4h = €1,000.

The most common Ryanair refusal: "technical issue"

Ryanair frequently refuses claims citing a "technical issue" or "aircraft malfunction". This is not a valid defence. The CJEU's Wallentin-Hermann ruling (2008) was clear: standard mechanical failures are part of the airline's normal operations and do not constitute extraordinary circumstances.

If Ryanair refuses for a technical reason, the refusal is contestable in court. Robin des Airs and other specialised services win these cases routinely, including in small claims court.

How to claim against Ryanair (DIY)

  1. Gather evidence: booking reference (PNR), boarding pass, screenshots of delay notifications, photo of the arrival time at destination (departure board or your watch).
  2. Submit via myRyanair: the online claim form on Ryanair.com. Attach all evidence.
  3. Wait 30 days for a reply. Often Ryanair offers a voucher first — refuse and demand cash.
  4. If refused or no reply: escalate to the Irish Aviation Authority (IAA) or the National Enforcement Body (NEB) of the departure country.
  5. Last resort: small claims court in the country where Ryanair operates (often the UK or Ireland have favourable jurisdictions).

Specific Ryanair scenarios

Cancellation with rerouting next day

Ryanair cancels your flight and books you on tomorrow's: if you arrive 3h+ after the original schedule, full €250-400 compensation due (in addition to the rerouting).

Missed connection because of Ryanair delay

If your Ryanair leg made you miss a connecting flight (booked separately or together), and your total delay at final destination exceeds 3 hours, compensation is calculated on the total distance to your final destination, not just the Ryanair leg.

Ryanair "voluntary cancellation" before 14 days

If Ryanair cancels more than 14 days before the flight, no compensation is due — only a refund or alternative flight.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Ryanair covered by EC 261?
Yes. Ryanair is an EU-licensed airline (Ireland). EC 261/2004 applies to all its flights departing from an EU airport or arriving in the EU operated by Ryanair. Same rules as Air France, Lufthansa, KLM — low-cost does not mean low-rights.
How much compensation am I entitled to on Ryanair?
€250 for flights up to 1,500 km, €400 for flights 1,500-3,500 km (or any intra-EU >1,500 km), €600 for flights over 3,500 km. Per passenger. Conditions: arrival delay of 3 hours or more, no extraordinary circumstances.
How do I claim against Ryanair?
Use Ryanair's online claim form (myRyanair). Attach booking confirmation, boarding pass and proof of delay (email, screenshot of departure board, photo of arrival time). What if Ryanair refuses my claim citing technical issues?

How long do I have to claim against Ryanair?
It depends on the country: 6 years in the UK, 5 years in France, 3 years in Germany, 2 years in Italy, 2 years in Spain. Claim as soon as possible — evidence ages, and Ryanair's databases are purged.
