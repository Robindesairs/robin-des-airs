02

Step 1: the WhatsApp submission

Before your call, the client has already chatted with the bot on WhatsApp. The bot captured:

  • The flight number → the bot proposes the route (departure → arrival).
  • A first eligibility check: delay ≥ 3h? distance? jurisdiction? within the time limit?
  • If potentially eligible → the bot asks for the documents.

Your role here: you step in when the client doubts, stalls or drops off. A human call in Wolof revives a file that would have been lost. You are the safety net of the journey.

03

The 3 documents to collect

No documents, no file. Your job: get them — legible and complete.

🎫

The ticket / booking

Shows the route, the date, the booking reference and all passengers.

  • ✓e-ticket, airline or agency e-mail
  • ✕a blurry screenshot of a text
🪪

The boarding pass

Proves the client actually took the flight. The strongest proof.

  • ✓paper OR the version in the airline's app
  • ✕thrown away? → we recover it (tip below)
🛂

The passport

For identity and the mandate. Used only for the file, and protected.

  • ✓full photo page, sharp
  • ✕never shared publicly

📸 A good photo: the whole document in frame, well lit, sharp text, laid flat, no finger on the text. Avoid: blur, cropped, glare, too dark.

💡"Lost boarding pass" tip: never block the client. It can often be recovered from the airline's app or the check-in e-mail. Failing that, the ticket + proof of travel can be enough — always look for a solution.

🧾 Bonus (care expenses): if the client was stuck a long time and paid for hotel, taxi or meals, tell them to keep the receipts — that money comes back on top of the compensation, and it does not reduce it. (More in Module 3.)

04

Checking a file

⚖️ The golden rule

The AI pre-checks the documents, but a human confirms. Never the AI alone on legal documents.

Before you validate, check 4 things:

  • Is the photo legible (whole, sharp)?
  • Is the name the same on the passport AND the ticket?
  • Does the flight match the one announced?
  • Do all passengers on the file have their documents?

If a document is bad: ask for a re-take — politely, explaining why. E.g.: "The photo is a bit blurry, could you re-take it flat with more light? 🙏"

⏱️24h SLA both sides: the team validates in under 24h, and the client re-sends a rejected document in under 24h. A file that moves fast is a file that pays out.

05

The mandate (the signature)

It's the authorisation that lets Robin des Airs claim the compensation in the client's name from the airline.

How to present it and reassure:

  • It is not a commitment to pay: we do the work. £0 / €0.
  • Online signature, simple, from the phone (we send the link).
  • The client keeps a 14-day withdrawal right.

🚫Red-line reminder (Module 1): you give no legal advice on the mandate's clauses. A pointed legal question → "our team will explain it." You are not a lawyer.

06

Bank details

🔒NEVER bank details on WhatsApp. And never before the claim is won. If anyone asks for bank details on WhatsApp, it's not us.

So when? Once the file is accepted, then once the funds are received from the airline → we ask for the bank details via a secure link (form), and we transfer the client's share within 5 working days.

The anti-scam line to say: "We will never ask for your bank details on WhatsApp, nor for money. We are the ones who pay you."

07

The 6 tricky objections

Base answers — adapt with your own words. Stay calm and clear: an objection is just a question in disguise. (Wolof line for Gambian / Wolof-speaking clients.)

"Too good to be true… it's a scam"
EN

"It's not a scam. The money goes into your account, and you pay nothing whatever happens."

WO

« Du arnaque. Xaalis bi ci sa compte lay dem, te doo fey dara lu mu mën a doon. »

"Why do you take 25%?"
EN

"The 25% is our work: we do everything for you. And if we recover nothing, you pay nothing."

WO

« 25 % bi mooy sunu liggéey : nuy def yëf yépp ci sa wàll. Bu fekkee jotunu dara, doo fey dara. »

"I lost my boarding pass"
EN

"No worries. We can recover it from the airline's app or your check-in e-mail."

WO

« Bul jëf. Mën nanu ko wut ci appli compagnie bi walla ci email enregistrement bi. »

"I'm worried about my passport"
EN

"Your passport is used only for your file, we protect it, it is never shared publicly."

WO

« Sa passeport, ci sa dossier rekk lanu koy jëfandikoo, danu koy aar, du génn ci mbedd. »

"How long will it take?"
EN

"If the airline cooperates, 2–3 months; if it resists, longer. But you do nothing — we keep you posted."

WO

« Bu compagnie bi déggoo gaaw, ñaari–ñetti weer ; bu dëgëree, mën na yagg. Waaye yaw doo def dara, danu lay xamal. »

"The airline offered me a voucher"
EN

"Don't accept the voucher! You're entitled to cash, not a voucher. Leave it to us, we'll recover your compensation."

WO

« Bul nangu bon d'achat bi ! Am nga droit ci xaalis (cash), du bon. Bàyyil nu ko, danu lay jotal sa indemnité. »
