01

Welcome to Robin des Airs

Our reason for being, in one line:

We take from the airlines, we give back to families.

The problem we solve. When a flight is badly delayed, cancelled, or a passenger is denied boarding, the airline owes them money (it's the law). But airlines never say so, the process is slow, and people don't know their rights.

Our job. We recover that money on the client's behalf, so they don't have to fight.

🤝 Our promise — repeat it to every client

  • ✓£0 / €0 upfront. Never a bank card, never money asked.
  • ✓We take 25% only if we win. The client keeps 75%.
  • ✓No win, no fee. If we recover nothing, the client pays nothing.

Why these markets & the diaspora. Flights from Lagos, Abuja, Accra, Entebbe and Banjul to Europe & the UK are long and often disrupted, and families travel a lot. Yet nobody ever told them they could be owed up to £520 / €600. That's where we help — and where we speak their language.

What you are NOT: not a lawyer, not a solicitor (see section 06 — this is critical). You are a claims & recovery specialist.

03

The product in plain words: EU261 & UK261

Two sister laws that force airlines to compensate passengers — one for the EU (€), one for the UK (£) since Brexit.

The amounts (per person)

Flight distanceEU261 (€)UK261 (£)
Short — ≤ 1,500 km€250£220
Medium — 1,500 to 3,500 km€400£350
Long — > 3,500 km€600£520

Which law applies? (key for our markets)

The flight…LawExample
to / from the UKUK261 (£) · regulator UK CAALagos → London on British Airways / Virgin
via the EU or on an EU airlineEU261 (€)Accra → Amsterdam on KLM · Lagos → Paris on Air France · Banjul/Entebbe → Brussels

🌍 For our markets: Lagos, Abuja, Accra, Entebbe and Banjul to Europe / the UK are all > 3,500 km = the top tier → €600 or £520.

When the passenger is entitled

The flight must have had one of these: a delay of 3h+ on arrival, a cancellation (told less than 14 days before), or denied boarding (overbooking).

AND the flight must be covered: departing the EU or UK (any airline), or arriving in the EU/UK on an EU/UK airline.

AND: it must not be an "extraordinary circumstance" (extreme weather, external strike, security…), and it must be within the time limit (a few years — about 5 in the EU, up to 6 in the UK).

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Several people on one booking

Each passenger has their own claim. A family of 4 adults on a delayed Lagos–London = 4 × £520 = £2,080. → Always ask how many people travelled on the same booking.

The client: who they are, what they feel

Either they live in the UK / Europe and travel home, or they're at home heading to Europe / the UK. Often diaspora. Here are their fears — and how to defuse them:

What they thinkYour answer

05

Call scripts — English (& Wolof)

These are a base. Use your own natural words. For Nigeria/Ghana, a little friendly Pidgin is fine; for Gambia, switch to Wolof (scripts below each step).

a · Opening — always greet before getting to the point
EN

"Good morning! How are you? How is the family? My name is [Name], I'm calling from Robin des Airs."

WO

« Asalaa maalekum ! Naka nga def ? Ana waa kër ga ? Maa ngi tudd [Name], maa ngi jóge Robin des Airs. »

b · Introduce Robin des Airs
EN

"Robin des Airs is a company that helps people whose flight was delayed or cancelled to recover the money the airline owes them."

WO

« Robin des Airs mooy entreprise bu di dimbali ñi seen avion yéex walla ñu annuler ko, ngir ñu jot seen xaalis ci compagnie bi. »

c · The "£0 / €0 upfront" & the 25% — the most important
EN

"You don't pay anything upfront. If we recover the money, we take 25% and you keep 75%. If we recover nothing, you pay nothing."

WO

« Doo fey dara ci sa bopp. Bu fekkee danu jot xaalis bi, danu jël 25 %, yaw nga jël 75 %. Bu fekkee jotunu dara, doo fey dara. »

d · Reassure "this is not a scam"
EN

"It's not a scam. We work for your right, and the money goes into your account — not ours."

WO

« Du arnaque. Danuy liggéeyal sa droit, te xaalis bi, ci sa compte lay dem, du ci sunu bopp. »

e · Stating eligibility — always with care
EN

"From what you're telling me, your flight could be eligible for compensation, but we'll check it carefully before we confirm."

WO

« Ci li nga ma wax, sa vol bi mën na am indemnité, waaye danu koy seetlu bu baax balaa nu la ko wóoral. »

⚠️

Never "you'll definitely win." Always "potentially / we check."

f · Closing + asking for a review (only if it went well)
EN

"Thank you for your trust. If you were happy with this, a Google review helps us a lot — I'll send you the link."

WO

« Jërëjëf ci sa kéwél. Su la neexoon, mën nga nu bind benn avis ci Google, dafay dimbali nu lool. Damay yónnee la lien bi. »

06

The red line — never cross it

Why this is vital: giving paid "legal advice" or posing as a lawyer is restricted — in France and the UK alike. If we cross the line, we risk a lot. We stay in our lane: claims recovery.

✅ You CAN

  • ✓Say whether a file is eligible or not, and why.
  • ✓Give the amounts and explain the rules.
  • ✓Say "potentially eligible, we'll check".

❌ You must NEVER

  • ✕Present yourself as a lawyer or solicitor.
  • ✕Guarantee that the client will win.
  • ✕Give bespoke legal advice.
  • ✕Ask for money / bank details on WhatsApp / a "tip".
  • ✕Say we represent the client in court — a partner lawyer takes over (at our cost).

🗣️ The right words: "claims & recovery specialist", "our team", "we'll check", "potentially".

Banned: "lawyer / solicitor", "I guarantee", "legal opinion", "I'll represent you in court". A question outside EU261/UK261 (divorce, other dispute) → "that's for a lawyer".
